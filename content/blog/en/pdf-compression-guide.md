PDF files can become quite large, especially when they contain high-resolution images or many pages. Large PDFs can be difficult to email, upload to websites, or store. PDF compression solves this problem effectively.

## Why Compress PDFs?

- To stay within email attachment size limits
- To improve your website's loading speed
- To save storage space
- To speed up document sharing

## Method 2: Compress PDF with Adobe Acrobat

Adobe Acrobat Pro offers advanced compression options:

1. Open your PDF in Adobe Acrobat Pro
2. Go to "File" > "Optimize PDF"
3. Configure compression settings
4. Click "OK" and save the optimized PDF

## Method 3: Online Compression Tools

Tools like SmallPDF or ILovePDF are popular but upload your files to their servers. Suitable for non-sensitive documents.

## PDF Compression Tips

### For Best Results:
- **Optimize images before** adding them to the PDF
- **Remove unnecessary pages** before compressing
- **Check font embedding** options

### Which Compression Level to Choose?
- **Low Compression**: For documents requiring maximum quality
- **Recommended Compression**: Ideal balance for daily use
- **Extreme Compression**: When maximum size reduction is needed
